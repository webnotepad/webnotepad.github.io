Game strategy

Tips to play smarter
in every word game.

Whether you play Scrabble, Wordle, Words With Friends, or crosswords, these strategies help you get more from your letters.

🎲 Scrabble Strategy Tips

  • Always check for 7-letter words first — they earn a 50-point bonus for using all tiles (a "bingo").
  • High-value letters (Q, Z, X, J) are worth 8–10 points — use them on Triple Letter or Triple Word squares.
  • Keep a balanced vowel-to-consonant ratio on your rack (ideally 2–3 vowels).
  • Two-letter words (AA, AE, OE, etc.) are essential for parallel plays and tight board positions.
  • Sort results by score and look for plays that also set up future moves.

💡 Wordle Tips

  • Start with a word that contains common letters: CRANE, SLATE, AUDIO, or RAISE are popular openers.
  • Use the "Contains" filter to find words that include a confirmed letter (yellow tile).
  • Use "Starts With" and "Ends With" filters once you've confirmed positional letters (green tiles).
  • Eliminate as many letters as possible in your first two guesses to narrow the solution space.
  • Common 5-letter word endings: -TION, -ING, -ER, -LY, -ED, -EST.

🔤 Common Word Patterns

  • -ING — adding to verb roots gives long, high-scoring words.
  • -TION / -SION — common endings for 6–8 letter words.
  • UN- / RE- / IN- — prefixes that extend short words dramatically.
  • -LY — quick adverb suffix that works with many adjectives.
  • -ER / -EST — comparative and superlative endings for easy length boosts.
  • QI, ZA, JO, XU — short valid Scrabble words for high-value letters.
